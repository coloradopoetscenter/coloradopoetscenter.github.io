Lapis Luzuli 2012

In a dream
I have heard the songs

of story tellers

miners out of ancient Asia
who worked the banks

of a river far north

who rubbed the “stone of heaven”

crooning softly
prayers and praises

until all their verses
turned into azure

streaked with gold.

And we who scoff
at holy things

making mere adornment
out of sacred stones

we feel no sad strain
running through

our hearts

hearts stained
with a different hue

midnight blue

that fades to black.
O lapis, heal us

open our minds

strengthen our vision

bring us to the river
of your source

old river reborn
in gold and blue

under a cloudless
sun drunk sky.

Though we gaze upon
a world

turned blood red
and gun barrel gray

may our timeworn eyes
be glittering

and gay.
