Dancers

 (This poem was inspired by the 1957 movie, Funny Face, a film that starred Fred  Astaire and Audrey Hepburn.)

          The little digital disk
spins and spins

          and the two figures

          ever shining, ever alive

          fill the screen with light
and movement.

          He loves and she loves
          and they love

the lovers leap
off the screen

          the white doves
take flight

          around and above them

          gliding swans offer their grace

sheltering tress

          bend and sway
to the lovers’ dance.

          The entire scene--

          whirling dervish lovers

birds gliding
or soaring in delight

          deep green bank, radiant stream

distant chapel--

          all of it dances off
the spinning disk

out of the enclosing screen

          leaving time and place
and Ixion fate behind

          entering its own blessed space.

We who watch
exulting in the joy--

the wounded and weak
the afflicted and ill

the bitter and broken--

are all of us

dancers.
