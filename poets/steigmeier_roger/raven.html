Raven's Old World

(My response to Aldous Huxley’s Brave New World)

Mustapha Mond
would not know this place

with its low hanging

haunted moon

always a bright orange

and its mysterious soft light
just at the crest of a hill

of tall pines.

Mond would not believe
what lies beyond

his dystopic domain:

 

the little villages
set among vast fields

of wild lavender
and rosemary

settlements of
mountain men

wiccan women

remnants of lost tribes

poets and dreamers

all living free
and in peace

their villages
in a hidden valley

shielded by cloud shrouded
peaks.

 

The soft murmur of voices
heard at midnight

is not coming through
head phones

to massage the minds
of sleeping children

with its conditioning message
endlessly repeated;

it is the unison chant
of a coven

casting a healing spell
for a wounded heart.Â 

 

Mustapha Mond
does not know this place

but it knows him

watches him

through the eye of a raven.

Raven looks upon this
great leader and teacher

and makes a mocking cry:

Ka ka ka!

Mond, you make me laugh
but that was always

my intention.

I guess I’ll let this madness
go on just a bit longer.

I’ve gotten a big kick
out of this very elaborate trick

but all things under the sun
must come to an end.

The hatcheries hum

soma is dropped

couples happily copulate

castes perform
their tasks

according to their rank

and station.

Just beyond the barriers
of faux Eden

a north wind rises

the old orange moon
grows brighter

covens hold hands

close their eyes

and cast a spell.

Mond stirs in his bed
troubled by a strange dream

of laughing corvids.
