Woody

Inasmuch as you have done it unto the least of these, my brethren, you have done it unto me.

Cleave a piece of wood, lift up a rock; and I am there.

Old slouch hat

guitar on his back

dustbowl troubadour

riding the rails from sundown
to sunrise.

Once upon a time

some suits with sponsors
to please

put him in a radio studio
and told him to behave.

Well, hell,Â 
you can’t stick

a wild wood thrush
in a cage!

 

There’s a long freight
slow running

from a California yard

and it’s going ‘cross the country

as the afterglow
of daylight

looms like painted fire
before the firmament

is starred.

He’s perched atop
an empty grain car

singing for all those folks
who have nothin’

but a prayer

a wood thrush
with the sunset

in his eyes

patron saint

of hobo camps
and shanty towns

vagabond poet

bound for glory.
