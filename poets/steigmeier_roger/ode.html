Ode To Letting Go

(A poetic impression of Gustav Mahler’s 10th Symphony Finale)

O joyous dirge

whose pungent drum

beats time

and place

and person;

the face of fate
straining

to be effaced.

Plangent drum

so slow the march

the nearly arrested procession

reaper’s gay profession.

A distant lark unseen
sings a portent

of grand detachment.

 

Quivering memory

flickering yesteryears

and the flashing images
strike like lightening

through heavy clouds
of cluttered recollection.

The images will not stay

they fade, they fade
into deepening darkness.

The darkness does not hold
it, too, begins to fade

and the clear dusk revealed

is a sunrise

of grateful surrender.

Soft magenta sky

soul of repose

but then a sudden burst
of primal light;

the whole dear Earth
is given up

let go

and what rises
in beatitude

is spirit out of flesh

the life

beyond goodness

or glory.
