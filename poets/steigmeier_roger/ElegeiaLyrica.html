Elegeia Lyrica

(Three Aspects of Gaia)

She stands alone
a threnody

draped in black

amidst ruined shrines
in silhouette.

Echoes of flutes
drift out

into darkness

linger under
an iron moon

then fade away.

She stands alone
a hymn gowned

in lavender

under the arch
of an ancient chapel

her arms opened wide
to receive

the voices of children

risen out of earth

children of lost tribes

PAGANI PERDIDAS

tribes of the moon

tribes of the willows

tribes slaughtered.

She stands alone
cantata in deep green

mourning doves
and nightingales

circling her head
sibilant with song.

Rarely glimpsed
in the chill depth

of long night
descendants
of lost tribes

Gaians hide behind
guardian redwoods

millennial sentinels.

They watch.

They hope.
