Enya

In a cathedral of trees

in the hush of evening

a nightingale sings
to the distant stars

and the song is answered
by celestial voices.

Her reflection appears
and vanishes

like a face seen
in a mirror

in an instant

of summer lightning.

She is like her voice

ethereal, elusive

a raven’s shadow
on a pale white curtain

or the faint aroma
of old lavender

released from

an antique music box.

 

There is a black lace shawl
thrown across a chair

but where has she gone?

She is gone to the mountains
and the hidden waterfall

to the forest groves

and the green rocks
high above the sea.

She has gone to gaze again

at the constellation
of the Seven Sisters

and to dream once more

of the old time

when the goddess danced
amidst bright garlands

under a full silver moon.
