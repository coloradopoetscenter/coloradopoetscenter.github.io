--- layout: poet title: Beryle Williams : Just Seeing ---

Just Seeing

I’m just sitting here

reading some poetry

it doesn’t matter whose

and I look out

the north window

not to see really

but to contemplate some lines

I’ve just read

and this deer a doe

is suddenly there

on the slope near the house

quietly munching

the dry late November grass

washed by rays of low sun

deep tan with highlights

of buff and gold

the same color as the deer yes

moving now through the grass

like a soft wind

becoming grass and then

deer again

and I’m struck

with the thought hard

like with a rock nudged

off the hill there Why

me how can I be so blessed

to see this

just sitting here with no effort

and I sit still

no longer contemplating

anything

but just seeing and seeing

really seeing

deer

 

(Reprinted with thanks, from Animals in Poetry 2000, Sarasota Poetry Theatre Press, 2001)
