The Monarchs

When the butterflies lifted

in fall, a sudden audible fluttering

of hundreds of wings

from the circle of pines

that will remain

forever magic

for their resting there

 

they were not for pinning down

like chloroformed specimens

to the poem

 

were not for catching

in any net

but the holy eye

of the mind, migrating

out of our heads

on the wind of our breath

of oh and ah

into a country

where words would wander

helpless as bats

in stark sunlight

were they to try

to follow.

 

It is because of them,

butterflies fed since birth

on namesake

of the God of medicine,

the healing milkweed,

because of those Monarchs at dusk

I know now

I will never write

the real butterfly poem

 

nor will you write it

though, believe me,

I wished it for you

or just for the sake

of the hungry, bony

Word

but it will not be

fleshed out in this way.

 

We cannot release

what cannot be contained

 

the poem

that refuses the rule

of words,

flushed only

for an incredible moment

from its secret resting place

before migrating back

to that wild country

 

where nothing can pursue it

save by facing the perils

of passing through,

a risk of becoming

Poem

to touch it

 

where words, like ourselves

are stopped

at the primitive boundaries

silent

and full of light

from its passing.

 

(First Prize in AAUW Poetry Contest, reprinted with thanks form The Crisp Pine, AAUW Press, Saint Paul, 1990)
