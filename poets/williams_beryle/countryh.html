Growing Old in Cold Country

--for my mother

 

It is less difficult than she thought

in small changes   a few dark hairs

turning gray   a few more

slowly changing to white   the eyes

narrowing at the corners

the nose too

thinner    sharper

quick to catch scent

of shifts in the weather

 

 

Soon she will be

the white fox running

across vast expanses of white

No one will see her

No one will guess she has gone or suspect

she has carried away the breath of their world

 

 

When they sense their loss

there will be no trace    her footprints

white on white drifted in

with white    They will go blind

searching     and what wind they have left

for the chase will be sucked from them

 

 

weak jet streams on the tail of her scent

slowly dissolving

in thin    white    air

 

(reprinted with thanks from Mourning Our Mothers: Poems About Loss, Andrew Mountain Press, 1998)
