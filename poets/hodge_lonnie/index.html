Biography

Lonnie Hodge, MFA, Ph.D., is a poet, playright, professor, cultural consultant in Asia and a free-lance writer. Born in Pueblo, Colorado, his works have appeared in hundreds of regional and international publications since 1960, including Appalachia, The Bridge, Eleventh Muse, Kansas Quarterly, as well as over two dozen anthologies. He is a past recipient of a National Endowment for the Arts Fellowship in Poetry and an Artists Residency at the Millay Colony. He is currently a visiting professor in China and a consultant on Chinese cultural issues for International business concerns. He is fluent in several Asian languages and lectures on Asian literature. He is a graduate of Vermont College, UCCS and others...

His next book: Expats Syndrome , a collection of essays on China,  will be published by Xiao Huo Press, Autumn 2005.
