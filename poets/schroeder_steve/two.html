Two Minutes at the Dillingham Airport

The sun afloat in a deep river

blue as the Kanektok must be—

 

Tinged with the tang of salmon,

airflows tickle tame noses—

 

A Grumman Goose pointing

down the gravel taxi strip,

 

A great groaning waterfowl

to carry the few intrepid souls—

 

Toward the leisurely reel of skyline,

where mountains and forests surely

 

Open apertures, eyes, and horizons,

a husky/wolf hybrid pulls its owner.

 The sun afloat still where it was,

anchored in current overhead—

 

A bush plane buzzing and biting,

a horsefly in the feathers—

 

Concrete on terminal walls

grayer than the Kanektok

 

Flashes unlike the sides of coho salmon

landed thrashing on a hook—

 

Outside the only gate an Inuit drinks

from a paper bag and watches

 

Arrivals and departures not seeing

each other for even a minute.
