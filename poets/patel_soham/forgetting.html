Forgetting

after Adrienne Rich

   Resting on the sand are the shards of our faiths.
Yesterday the answer for our grief emerged
   herself out from a wave's break.
Like the surprise of a
   newborn's gurgle, like we've never heard it
  before.
Last night I read another line by Dickinson.
   She must have been fully aware of
      everything she suffered
from a resurgence of the mindhand
  switch to the want for longing.  Her love graining
the years into the fragments
  we've come to collect.
And she kept it all until the last
  day, dashing her memory
  for the sake of the sands,
  holding questions in her hand
  and capitalizing tomorrows on Truth.
Her memory came from the same
    source each morning.
