Alice B. Toklas Confronts a Tea Bag

Alice B. Toklas Confronts a Tea Bag

27 Rue de Fleurus, 1948

 

Gertrude Stein would not approve.

Gertrude Stein is dead.

Gertrude Stein doesnot approve.

You can not still Gertrude Stein. Alice B. Toklas knows that. Gertrude Stein lives on by the force of her words, and her words assert her disapproval.

 

A tea bag? says Gertrude Stein. Is that one word or two? No matter, says Gertrude Stein. Tea bag is a very unpleasant image. Take it away. Toute suite!

 

Oh dear, says Alice B. Toklas as she regards what the Lipton Company has done.

Then she asks herself this question: Would Picasso use a tea bag?

 

Wearing a tea bag over each ear, Alice B. Toklas prepares her pot the conventional way.
