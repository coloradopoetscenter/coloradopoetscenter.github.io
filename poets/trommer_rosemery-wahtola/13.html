13.7 Billion Years in the Making

Bless the body, its belly of moon,

and bless the electric pulse coursing through it

that links us back to a bigger bang.

 

Bless each long finger and each stubby toe,

the long arms for reaching, the legs for dancing

and the smooth cupped yes of breast,

 

the thin hairs that stand at attention when thrilled or cold

and the skin that stretches like praise across the living bones.

Bless the tongue for its pink curiosity

 

and the back of the knee for its vulnerability.

Bless the long spine, that sterling strand where desire meets curve,

and the ornery elbow that knows how to hurt.

 

And what is it in us that knows how to laugh?

Bless that, and the ridiculous brain that wants to control,

and bless the tears that well up whenever they want to

 

reminding us that we’ve come from water,

that every movement of the body baptizes us into the moment

and that gratitude is our birthright.

 

(from Insatiable, Sisu Press, 2004)
