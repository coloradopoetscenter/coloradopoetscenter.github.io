Sonnet

O my luve’s like a red, red rose

That’s newly sprung in June.

— Robert Burns

 

Nothing about my love is like a red,

red rose that’s newly sprung in June. A rose

is difficult to care for, never grows

unless it’s pruned and doted on and fed

all kinds of chemicals. And now they’ve bred

the smell right out of most of them—the nose

gets no sweet whiff when sniffing right up close.

And roses don’t last long. Just blink, they’re dead.

 

My love is much more like a rabbit brush—

impossible to kill. For in this spell

of drought, its tufts still wave like numinous

gold hands that greet you from the field. And crush

the slender leaves between your fingers—smell

their tang. Yes, rabbit brush. My luve's robust.

 

(from Insatiable, Sisu Press, 2004)
