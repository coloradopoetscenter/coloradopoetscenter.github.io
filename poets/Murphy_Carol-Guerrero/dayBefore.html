Day  Before

My father opens his eyes and asks           Is this death?

He fingers the arm of his wheelchair.
He hears someone breathing hard, a raspy sucking.
Himself. 

He sees roses, sun,  our faces.
Air strokes his pale hair, smoothes it back .

No.                           We tell him no.

He says he has walked through long halls trying doors
in a labyrinth.
Does he seek the death room?
Earlier he had called out       eyes closed    voice breaking open our quiet

 Did you lose me?  I thought you had lost me.

In this room, the rose garden at the VA hospital,
his doctor offers to his mouth a wild strawberry from her garden
so ripe my father crushes it with his trembling lips,
the only food he takes, smear of red juice, summer sweet.

Then again       Is this death   he asks.
We think it is death’s
opposite, this life        ours        our electric bodies
leaning into each other
in the only room we remember.
